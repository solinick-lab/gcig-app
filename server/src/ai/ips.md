# Investment Policy Statement — The Griffin Fund (GCS Investment Group)

## Executive Summary
The Grace Church School Investment Group (GCS Investment Group) is a student-led initiative that actively manages and grows a designated amount of capital through strategic trading and investment activities, while simultaneously educating students about various investment strategies and financial markets to develop practical investment skills across the student body. The Investment Policy Statement (IPS) outlines the governance, investment theories, and portfolio management decisions of the GCS Investment Group.

## Objectives
The GCS Investment Group strives to educate as well as excite future generations of Grace students about investing and we aim to educate members about different investment strategies that are being used in the professional world.

## Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to provide a framework for the GCS Investment Group's portfolio management. The IPS establishes guidelines on permitted investments and outlines the process for transitioning portfolio responsibility between student leaders.

## Governance of the GCS Investment Group
The Advisory Board, appointed by the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) of Grace Church School, Susan Austin, is composed of professionals with expertise in finance or a commitment to supporting the mission of the GCS Investment Group. The Advisory Board advises students on investment decisions and offers guidance on investment selection.

The GCS Investment Group shall have a Faculty Advisor who ensures that the IPS is upheld and provides oversight of the GCS Investment Group's day-to-day activities.

The CFO executes trades on behalf of the GCS Investment Group in accordance with the IPS and serves as the primary liaison between Goldman Sachs and the GCS Investment Group.

## Student Roles and Responsibilities
**Presidents** oversee the operations of the club and serve as voting members of the GCS Investment Group.

**Chief Investment Officer (CIO)**, similar to the Presidents, manages club operations but more actively oversees the Portfolio Managers and guides equity selections. The CIO also serves as a voting member.

**Portfolio Managers** are responsible for reviewing Traders' presentations, approving them after evaluation, and pitching final recommendations on whether to buy or sell equities. They may also pitch equities to the voting members.

**Traders** are the most junior members of the GCS Investment Group, and the initial role for all new members of the GCS Investment Group. They may pitch equities to the voting members after receiving feedback and approval from a Portfolio Manager.

## Investment Guidelines and Restrictions
In any decision of an investment, the following factors must be factored in, if relevant:
1. General economic conditions
2. The possible effect of inflation or deflation
3. The expected tax consequences, if any, of investment decisions or strategies
4. The role that each investment or course of action plays within the investment/management strategy of GCS Investment Group's overall portfolio
5. The past performance, risk attributes, liquidity, and market correlation of any Non-Cash Investment, as well as any management or similar fees charged in respect of such Non-Cash Investment
6. An asset's special relationship or special value, if any, to the purposes of the group

## Permitted Investments
- Equities that are publicly traded on the NYSE and NASDAQ
- Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)
- Money Market Funds or other cash equivalents
- Publicly Traded Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)
- Bond Mutual Funds
- Other assets that benefit the group in its mission and long-term investment goals require the approval of the Advisory Board as well as the voting members in a majority fashion.

## Prohibited Assets
- Derivatives (options, futures, swaps, forwards, etc.)
- Commodities or Cryptocurrency

## Target Asset Allocation Policy
- US equities can be no less than 20% and no more than 100%
- Cash or cash equivalents can be no less than 5% and no more than 100%
- Equities should generally be held for at least three months to encourage long-term investing and discourage short-term trading.
- If a position ever declines beyond 15%, the voting members must meet to review the holding and decide whether to maintain or sell the investment.
- There is a maximum 10% of the portfolio allocation to any single security. Exceptions can be made with approval from the Advisory Board and the voting members.
- Proper exposure to different sectors must take place to ensure protection from negative returns in a particular industry.
- The voting members will meet with the Advisory Board quarterly to discuss current market conditions as well as the allocation of the portfolio.

## Hiatus While Students Are Not in School
The fund will continue to hold the ETFs over the summer and will sell all of the group picks before summer starts. The funds will be invested in cash equivalents until a period when the GCS Investment Group decides to make an investment the following fall.

## Voting (per the IPS)
In the process of choosing an investment, the majority of voting members must agree to allocate capital to such an investment.

Voting members include:
- Presidents and CIO: One vote each
- Club Members: Two votes as a collective

The majority vote wins. In the scenario of a tie, the Presidents and CIOs will meet again to offer changes in vote. If it is still tied after the meeting, the Advisory Board will make the final decision.

No amendments shall occur to the IPS unless approved by the CFO and the Advisory Board.
