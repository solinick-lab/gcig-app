# Internal Club Policies — The Griffin Fund

## Introduction
This document provides a basis for most internal club activities. This includes promotions, voting, job descriptions for Analysts, Portfolio Managers, the CIO, and Presidents. This document also establishes what type of attendance is acceptable for all club members.

## Promotions
Any member of The Investment Club may be promoted, given they have shown interest in the club, have learned sufficient investment skills correlated with their current role, and have an acceptable attendance record. The role progression path is: **Junior Analyst → Analyst → Portfolio Manager → President**.

## Student Roles and Responsibilities
**Presidents** oversee the operations of the club and serve as voting members of the GCS Investment Group.

**Portfolio Managers** are responsible for reviewing Traders' presentations, approving them after evaluation, and pitching final recommendations on whether to buy or sell equities. They may also pitch equities to the voting members. Portfolio Managers have the responsibility of being the first point of contact with the Traders, and should be a positive example to the Traders. If Portfolio Managers deny a pitch made by a Trader, they must show why they denied it, and Portfolio Managers must have decency when denying a pitch.

**Analysts** are similar to Junior Analysts but can advise junior analysts on pitches and are also a step closer to portfolio managers.

**Junior Analysts** are the most junior members of the GCS Investment Group, and the initial role for all new members of the GCS Investment Group. They may pitch equities to the voting members after receiving feedback and approval from a Portfolio Manager. The traders are responsible for pitching most of the stock picks.

**Note:** A Portfolio Manager may be promoted to a voting member if all current voting members unanimously agree, following excellent attendance, good investment pitches, and time spent as a Portfolio Manager. A new role below the President will be created as determined by the voting members.

## Attendance
Attendance at all **Wednesday** club meetings is **mandatory**. If a member is found to be skipping Wednesday club meetings without adequate reasoning, there will be a conversation regarding their attendance. Wednesday meetings are where the most important votes and events happen, because the entire club will be there.

It is expected that there will be another meeting throughout the week, either on Tuesday or Thursday. This **non-club-block meeting** will have attendance, and the leaders of the club will use the attendance as a factor that goes into promotions.

Acceptable attendance to non-club-block meetings by role:
- **Presidents:** 100% attendance at all meetings.
- **Portfolio Manager:** 50–60% attendance at meetings.
- **Analysts:** 50–60% attendance at meetings.
- **Junior Analysts:** 30–40% attendance at meetings.
- **Inactive Analysts:** 0–30% attendance at meetings.

**NOTE:** If an individual wants to be promoted, they should show up to as many meetings as possible and prove themselves by actively participating in pitches and in voting sessions. You will not get promoted if you show up to the bare minimum of meetings.

## Voting (operational)
Voting is the task of deciding which stocks pitched by Traders are worth investing in.
- Each **President** has one vote.
- The **CIO** has one vote.
- Each **club member** votes in a general election. The general election is worth **three votes**.

The majority rules in the general election, and the three votes will be cast in either yea or nay. Both Portfolio Managers and Junior Analysts vote in the general election. Most Wednesdays will be devoted to voting and pitches.

**Note:** Members will only be allowed to vote if they were present for the pitch.

## Pitches
Pitches will be conducted every day the club meets, and voting will take place every day as well. Pitches should take no longer than **8 minutes**. In each pitch, the presenter should have:
- A clearly formatted presentation
- An obvious knowledge of the stock they picked
- An accurate portrayal of upside and downside

The stock pick should be in an **approved sector** and within the restrictions set out in the IPS. The stock in question should have:
- Good growth potential
- Good EBITDA flow
- Some protection from market fluctuations

We are using **endowment money**.
