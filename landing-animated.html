§ I · The Approach 01 / 04

Rigorous research.
Disciplined investing.
Collective decisions.

The Fund was founded in the autumn of 2023 on a single premise: that a group of committed high-school students, given real capital and a disciplined process, can do the work of serious investors.

We cover public equities across seven industry pods. We meet weekly. We present original research to our peers. We take every position to a weighted vote, and we keep a permanent record of what each of us has recommended and how it has performed. The Fund is an educational organization first — and an honest one.

01 — Research

Coverage by pod.

Members join one of seven industry pods, each led by a senior portfolio manager. Pods meet privately, decide what to research, divide the work, and present the resulting pitch to the general body together.

02 — Debate

Every position voted.

When a pitch is delivered, a weighted voting session opens. The general body's winning ballot carries extra weight alongside the officers'. Every member casts a ballot — Buy, Hold, or Sell — with a written reason.

03 — Record

An honest ledger.

Every approved pitch becomes a tracked position. Every call is kept on the record — what we bought, what we passed on, and how each has performed since. Accountability is built into the Fund, not bolted onto it.

“The purpose of the Fund is not to chase returns. It is to learn to think like investors — carefully, collaboratively, and on the record.”

From the charter, drafted September 2023
§ II · The Structure 02 / 04

Inside the Fund.

  • Portfolio

    A live portfolio, tracked per-lot.

    Positions, weightings, sector exposure, and risk metrics update on every page load. The Fund keeps a per-purchase cost basis so realised and unrealised performance can be attributed correctly back to the pitches that produced them.

  • Voting

    A weighted chamber, on the record.

    Every ballot carries a reason. Sessions auto-close at the posted deadline. Tied outcomes default to Hold. The result is a permanent record of how the Fund actually thought about each decision — not just what it decided.

  • Coverage

    A ledger of every call.

    The coverage page keeps a running record of every pitch and research report, the club's verdict, and the return since. It is the Fund's honest self-audit — and it is open to every member.

  • Risk

    A risk panel, visible to portfolio managers.

    Weighted beta, annualised volatility, max drawdown, concentration, and rebalancing flags. Available to portfolio managers and above, so the people making recommendations can see what those recommendations are doing to the book.

  • Attendance

    Meetings tracked, work recorded.

    Every meeting, every pitch, every ballot, every edit to the record is logged to an audit trail. Faculty advisors see the same ledger the members do.

§ III · Governance 03 / 04

A hierarchy of accountability, not of privilege.

The Fund is led by its President and its Chief Investment Officer. Senior portfolio managers lead the industry pods and the analysts within them. Every action that touches the portfolio — a pitch scheduled, a vote opened, a position recorded, a member's role changed — is written to an audit trail that cannot be edited after the fact.

Faculty advisors sit on the board as observers. They do not vote. They are there to ensure that the Fund is run responsibly, as an educational organisation, with the long-term interests of its members in mind. Members of the advisory board see the same record the members do, and they are free to question any decision the Fund has made.

The goal of the structure is not to distribute power. It is to distribute responsibility — so that when the Fund is right, the credit is collective, and when the Fund is wrong, the record is honest enough to learn from.
